Tripscape

Designing a landing page for a travel itinerary and guide platform

Tripscape is a platform that lets people create and store travel itineraries, write and discover travel guides, search for inspiration, and track how much of the world they've explored.

This page documents the process of designing Tripscape's landing page across 25 iterations — each exploring a different combination of layout, mood, visual style, audience, and tone — and explains how I arrived at a final design.

Chapter 5

Breaking Out — New Worlds, New Formats

I realized the previous iterations were all variations of the same underlying layout — just with different color palettes and card styles swapped in. From here, I challenged myself to start from scratch each time. What followed was an exploration of completely new formats: newspaper columns, manga panels, Japanese interiors, nature scenes, woodblock prints, and eventually full game UI reimaginings. Each iteration builds its own world with unique layouts, card shapes, and interaction metaphors.

Chapter 6

Refinement — Combining the Best Parts

After 21 wildly different iterations, patterns emerged in what I kept coming back to. I liked the bold sectioned layout from Superflat (20), the tactile polaroid and tape strip details from the scrapbook iterations (06/08), and the push-pinned guides. Time to stop exploring and start refining — pulling the best pieces into a cohesive design.

22

Refined

Sectioned + horizontal Warm & bold Scrapbook meets Superflat All travelers Confident & personal

Merged Superflat's bold sectioned layout with scrapbook polaroids, tape strips, and push pins. Bigger fonts, donut chart, AI only in hero and nav.
