Tripscape

Designing a landing page for a travel itinerary and guide platform

Tripscape is a platform that lets people create and store travel itineraries, write and discover travel guides, search for inspiration, and track how much of the world they've explored.

This page documents the process of designing Tripscape's landing page across 25 iterations — each exploring a different combination of layout, mood, visual style, audience, and tone — and explains how I arrived at a final design.

Chapter 1

Establishing a Baseline

Before exploring bolder directions, I started with a clean, neutral design to establish the core content hierarchy and messaging for Tripscape.

Clean Minimal

Single column Calm Minimal General Neutral

A safe starting point — black and white, sans-serif type, generous whitespace. The goal was to nail the content structure (hero, features, social proof, CTA) before layering on personality.

Chapter 2

Shifting to a Logged-In Experience

Iteration 01 was a marketing page for new users. But what does Tripscape actually feel like once you're using it? I shifted to designing the logged-in dashboard — the page travelers see every day.

02

Warm Explorer

Dashboard grid Cozy Travel journal Active travelers Personal

Earthy palette (terracotta, sand, forest green) with a card-based dashboard layout. Features an interactive SVG world map, itinerary and guide lists with sample data, and an explore section. Feels like opening a well-loved travel journal.

Chapter 3

Going Bold — Breaking the Mold

The first two iterations played it safe. Time to push into drastically different visual territory and see what happens when Tripscape gets personality. I also refocused the page hierarchy around itinerary creation as the core action, and introduced the AI Pro upsell.

03

City Pop

Full-width sections Fun & nostalgic 80s Japanese retro Young travelers Playful

Pink-to-purple gradient background, neon accents, palm tree sunset logo. Itinerary creation is front and center in the hero. Added an AI Trip Planner Pro upsell banner. The "Places Traveled" dashboard with a CSS donut chart, continent breakdown, and detailed world map SVG.

View iteration →
Chapter 4

Grounding It — Tactile and Personal

City pop was fun but maybe too loud. I wanted to explore whether Tripscape could feel like something physical — a leather notebook you carry on every trip. I also moved the AI feature out of the spotlight and made the world map full-width.

04

Traveler's Notebook

Single column Warm & tactile Leather journal Frequent travelers Personal

Cream paper background with subtle line texture, leather brown and stamp red palette. Globe-with-flight-path logo. Photo-style itinerary cards using CSS gradients. Full-width horizontal world map with detailed country shapes and continent breakdown. AI Planner tucked into a discreet dashed-border banner near the bottom.

View iteration →
Chapter 5

Retro With Purpose

Bringing back the Places Traveled dashboard from iteration 03 but as a full-width horizontal section. Trying a 60s/70s airline poster aesthetic — bold, geometric, teal and orange — to see if Tripscape can be playful and structured at the same time.

05

Retro Airline

Full-width hero Bold & fun 60s airline poster All travelers Confident

Dark nav bar, teal hero with orange stripe accents, cream background. Retro wing emblem logo. Itinerary cards have airport codes (NRT, PUQ, KEF, BER) and rounded corners. Full-width Places Traveled dashboard with donut chart, stats, continent links, and detailed horizontal world map. Pill-shaped buttons and tags throughout.

Chapter 6

Adding Depth — More Features, More Character

Previous iterations had the core sections but felt thin. I added a search bar, upcoming trip countdown, trending destinations, and recently saved content from the community. Also brought back the stats-left/map-right dashboard layout and went all-in on a scrapbook aesthetic.

06

Polaroid Scrapbook

Single column Warm & playful Scrapbook Creative travelers Personal

Cork board background, tilted polaroid-style itinerary cards with washi tape strips, camera shutter logo. New features: search bar, 12-day countdown to next trip, trending destinations row, and recently saved community content. Places Traveled has stats on the left and map on the right. No repeated stats in the hero.

Chapter 7

Your Trips as Boarding Passes

What if every itinerary felt like a ticket to somewhere? Styled the entire page around the metaphor of airline tickets and boarding passes — perforated tear lines, barcodes, departure/arrival routes, and a navy + red + sky blue palette.

07

Boarding Pass

Two-column cards Structured & fun Airline ticket All travelers Confident

Itineraries styled as boarding passes with dashed perforated lines, stub sections showing trip duration, and CSS barcode decorations. Ticket-stub plane logo. Stats-left map-right dashboard. Simplified high-level world map. 3 trending destinations with emojis. Navy and sky blue palette with red accents.

View iteration →
Chapter 8

The Best of Both — Mashup

Iterations 06 and 07 each had something great. What if the boarding passes were pinned to a corkboard? Added travel badges for personality and a bucket list for aspiration.

08

Corkboard Boarding Pass

Two-column cards Warm & structured Scrapbook + airline Active travelers Personal & fun

Cork background with tilted boarding pass cards held by tape strips, polaroid-style guide cards, and red push-pin bucket list. New features: passport-stamp badges (6 Continents, Marathon Runner, etc.) and a "Where to Next" bucket list. Combines the warmth of the scrapbook with the structure of the boarding pass.

Chapter 9

Simplify — Less Is More

Iteration 08 was too busy. Stripped out badges and bucket list, merged Trending + Explore into one "Discover" section with filter pills, moved the AI Planner next to the create button, and widened the layout. Terracotta and slate palette for Mediterranean warmth.

09

Terracotta Polaroid

Wide single column Warm & clean Mediterranean modern All travelers Inviting

Wider 1200px layout. Terracotta + slate palette. Polaroid itinerary cards with emojis over gradient backgrounds. AI Planner button grouped with Create Itinerary. Combined Discover section with filter pills (Trending, Itineraries, Guides, Destinations) mixing destinations and community content. Cleaner, fewer sections.

Chapter 10

A World of Its Own — Studio Ghibli

What if Tripscape felt like stepping into an animated film? Sky gradient at the top, grass in the middle, and a warm bulletin board for all the content — like a cozy cottage wall covered in travel memories. Full-width, tape strips, push pins, and a leaf logo.

10

Studio Ghibli

Full-width board Whimsical & warm Animated film Dreamers Magical

Fixed sky-to-grass gradient background, wooden nav bar, leaf-and-plane logo. Content sits on a full-width warm bulletin board with grid texture. Polaroid itineraries with colored tape strips, push-pinned guide cards and upcoming trip. Bucket list postcards with tape. Soft Ghibli-palette gradients (pinks, greens, blues).

Ghibli World

Scene + content Magical Illustrated landscape Dreamers Whimsical

Chapter 12

Starting Over — Showa Era Newspaper

Scrapped everything and started fresh. What if Tripscape looked like a vintage Japanese newspaper from the Showa era? Aged paper, monospace type, three-column layout, classified-ad style listings, halftone poster hero, and a completely different information hierarchy.

12

Showa Era

3-column newspaper Nostalgic Vintage print Culture lovers Editorial

Completely new design from scratch. Aged paper background, monospace + Georgia type, dark brown ink palette with crimson accents. Magazine masthead with volume/issue numbers. Sunset halftone hero poster. Three-column newspaper layout: itineraries as classified ads with stamp icons, guides as editorial entries, numbered bucket list. Full-width Places Traveled with oversized 41 stat. Ranked trending strip. AI in a dark utility bar.
